Meet Dr. Rumi Liyanagamage

Rumi has over 20 years of Australian General Practice. She is passionate about helping patients achieve good health. It is about social, emotional and physical wellness and not just absence of illness. She promotes healthy lifestyle, prevention and avoidance of complications of illnesses. The therapeutic relationship between the doctor and patient is the key to successful health outcomes.

Rumi studied medicine at the University of Adelaide on a merit scholarship awarded to her based on top high school year 12 results in Sri Lanka. She was the Dux of her year, in the leading National Girls’ School in Sri Lanka. She completed her internship in Addelaide, resident years in St Vincent Hospital Sydney, Eye Hospital Sydney, Armidale Base Hospital and Nepean Hospital. She worked in practices in western Sydney and Aboriginal medical service during her GP training. After her fellowship, she did a postgraduate diploma in child health at Westmead Children’s Hospital.

Rumi has been treating four generations of patients of the same family. Some of them travel very far to see her and have remained loyal. She equally enjoys seeing newborns, children, teenagers, parents and grandparents. She is married and has a daughter. She looked after her parents till they passed away. She is familiar with many barriers to seeking healthcare.

She has patients of different cultures, socioeconomic backgrounds and ethnicities. She has many indigenous, gender-diverse and patients with complex mental health issues. Everyone is treated with respect and empathy.

She takes time, listens and involves patients in the decision making. Rumi works with a trusted team of doctors, nurses, allied health professional and reception who share her work ethic. Continuity of care and preventive care is a strong pillar of good general practice.

Most of her patients with diabetes have good blood sugar control and avoid complications. Her patients with multiple chronic diseases are well looked after by coordinating care with specialists. Children are happy to see “Dr Rumi”. People with mental illness and disabilities feel listened to and supported.

In her free time, she meditates, practices Pilates and does yoga. She enjoys her time with her family.

Patient Advice

First visit as a new patient

Plan for at least a 30-minute appointment.

New “flu” symptoms

20 minutes if very unwell otherwise 10 minutes

Childhood health checks & vaccinations

Usually 30 mins with nurse than 20 mins with doctor – since includes a growth and developmental assessment.

*

List of various concerns

At least 20 minutes (40 minutes if a long list or if you save the most serious issue to the last – NOT advisable!)

*

“I need a referral”

For a condition that was thoroughly reviewed recently, 10 minutes; longer if new or more than 6 months since last review.
